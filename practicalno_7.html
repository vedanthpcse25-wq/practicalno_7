About: Rabindranath Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore was born on 7th May 1861 in Kolkata, India. He was a great poet, writer, philosopher, and artist. He became the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913 for his work "Gitanjali". Tagore played a key role in shaping Indian literature and music and composed the national anthems of India and Bangladesh. His thoughts on education and humanity continue to inspire generations.

Achievements

  • First Asian Nobel Laureate (1913)
  • Author of "Gitanjali"
  • Composed India's National Anthem "Jana Gana Mana"
  • Founder of Visva-Bharati University
  • Renowned poet, philosopher, and artist
  • Influenced Indian literature and music
